Welcome to the Bebras Review tool.

Guidelines on how to review tasks:

Login to Review system:

To review tasks, you need an SVN-account. If you still need to get one, please ask your national Bebras organizer to request one for you at mail bebras.org.

Go to https://review.bebras.org/ and log in with your SVN-credentials.

Go to Profile, and enter or change the number of tasks you would like to review.

Task review process:

Thank you for volunteering for reviewing tasks.

You may simply review the assigned tasks (the assignments are updated twice a week). You may also review any other tasks which are not from your own country. Some tasks may have multiple variants; you are encouraged to additionally review variants of the tasks you already reviewed.

Reviewing any task involves the following steps:

A. Writing an in-depth review and constructive feedback on the task. Please pay special attention to the task body, question & answer alternatives / interactivity description. The following template might help you.

  1. [IDEA] Is the idea original and attractive? Does solving the task require some computational thinking skills? Are the question & answer alternatives / interactive description well designed? What might you have done differently to improve the task?
  2. [TEXT] Is the text of the whole task clear and understandable by students without CS pre-knowledge? Are words and expressions used consistently in the text and question?
  3. [EXPL] Is the “explanation” section available and complete? Is there a clear reason why the correct answer is correct as well as why the wrong answers are wrong?
  4. [IT’S INF] Is the “It’s informatics” section available? Is it understandable by students of the appropriate level? Is it well connected to the task?
  5. [GRAPH] Are the figures clear? Are they CC licenced? Are they available in SVG format (i.e., as vector graphics)?
  6. [META] Are the metadata of the task available? (Such as: age, difficulty, computational thinking skills, author name(s) and email address(es)...etc.) Do the names of the task folder and file(s) comply with the prescribed format?
  7. [OTHER] Is there anything else that can be useful for the author or the community?

B. Rating the tasks based on the review you have given in two aspects:

  1. Its "as-of-now" state. (1 star is the worst rating, 6 stars is the best rating)
  2. Its "potential" state. (1 star is the worst rating, 6 stars is the best rating)

What makes a good bebras task:

Criteria for the quality of a task are available in the "Pre-Workshop Review Process" document (SVN/Documentation/General/taskPreparation/pre-workshop_review_process.pdf) and last year's presentation "What is a good Bebras task" (SVN/2019/Documents/Pre-Workshop-Workshop/What is a good bebras taks[ - Notes].pdf").
